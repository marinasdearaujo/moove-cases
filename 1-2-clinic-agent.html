The Owner's Side: One Panel for Schedule, Revenue and No-Shows

A barbershop was running on the owner's memory and a paper book. Appointments, walk-ins, which barber was free, who still owed for a product, all of it lived in someone's head, and the moment the shop got busy, bookings slipped and money leaked.

What we built

We put the AI booking agent on the front desk and gave the owner a single panel behind it. The agent books, confirms and reschedules on WhatsApp on its own (that conversation is the previous case), and everything it does lands in the owner's dashboard in real time: the day's schedule per barber, revenue, average ticket, and who did not show up. Staff, services, prices, commissions and product sales all live in one place instead of four notebooks.

The owner stopped rebuilding the schedule by hand and started reading one screen. Bookings stopped slipping, no-shows became visible, and the numbers that used to surface at month end now update as the day runs.
